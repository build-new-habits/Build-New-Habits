What is Alongside

Built around how you actually feel,
not how an algorithm thinks you should

Every day starts with a check-in. Energy, mood, sleep, pain. From there, Alongside offers three workout options and explains the reasoning behind each. You always choose. The app never tells you what to do — it coaches.

🎯
Listens first
A 60-second check-in captures energy, mood, sleep quality, and pain before a single recommendation is made. Your day shapes the session — not the other way round.
🔍
Explains itself
Every recommendation comes with a "why this?" explanation in plain English. No black-box prescriptions. No "trust the algorithm." You understand what's being suggested and why.

Why people abandon — and what Alongside does differently

Research identifies two abandonment phases: an initial rapid dropout driven by immediate disappointment, and a slower disengagement over weeks. Each reason maps directly to a design decision in Alongside.

The neurodivergent experience of fitness apps

Executive function challenges
Planning, initiating, and sustaining routines are neurologically harder for ADHD adults. It is not a willpower issue. It is a structural difference in how the brain manages initiation and task-switching.
Rejection sensitivity dysphoria
Many ADHD adults experience intense emotional responses to perceived failure. A broken streak or missed workout can trigger a shame spiral that ends engagement entirely — not temporarily. Permanently.
Demand avoidance
For many autistic users and some ADHD users, prescriptive instructions trigger avoidance responses. A single directive — "do this workout today" — can make engagement less likely, not more.
Variable energy
ADHD and autism commonly cause fluctuating energy states that mainstream apps cannot accommodate. An app that assumes consistent energy every day will fail these users every single time energy dips.

The gap in the literature — and why it matters

There is currently no large-scale study specifically measuring fitness app dropout rates among neurodivergent adults. There is robust evidence that shame-based environments are harmful to neurodivergent people. There is separate, robust evidence that fitness apps use shame-based mechanics. Nobody has formally connected these two bodies of research.

This is both a gap in academic literature and a significant commercial opportunity. Alongside is built to fill the product gap. A partnership with a university research team could generate the first dataset in this space — and position Build New Habits as the academic reference product for this population.

Design Philosophy

Why Alongside works the way it does

Every feature in Alongside is the result of a deliberate design decision rooted in evidence. Here are the five frameworks that underpin the product — and how they translate into what you actually experience.

SDT
Self-Determination Theory
Deci & Ryan, 1985
Three core psychological needs drive sustainable behaviour change: autonomy (you always choose), competence (exercises matched to your actual capacity, with your feedback recalibrating in real time), and relatedness (a consistent coach who addresses your specific situation). Alongside is designed to support all three simultaneously — because undermining any one of them kills long-term engagement.
SOLO
SOLO Taxonomy
Biggs & Collis, 1982
Alongside doesn't just give you workouts. It builds genuine movement literacy over time — from single exercises through complete sessions, to understanding how today connects to a goal, to eventually making your own informed movement choices. The coach's transparent rationale is the mechanism for this progression: you can't develop literacy if you never understand why.
MM
Mood Meter Science
Marc Brackett, Yale RULER Programme
Energy and mood are captured as independent axes — because you can be low energy but calm, or high energy but anxious, and these states need different coaching responses. Most apps treat "how are you feeling?" as a courtesy question. Alongside treats it as the most important data point of the day.
BRN
Burnout Research
Dr Claire Plumbly, 2024
Burnout doesn't arrive suddenly. It builds through a pattern of accumulated depletion that most fitness apps accelerate rather than detect. Alongside's pattern-detection engine monitors energy and mood trends across sessions and triggers Recovery Mode automatically — before burnout lands, not as a response to it.
ACT
Acceptance & Commitment Therapy
Hayes et al.
Movement is connected to personal values and meaning, not external performance pressure. Rest is validated as legitimate, not framed as failure. The app meets you in whatever state you arrive in — anxious, depleted, reluctant, or ready — and the response is always the same: you are exactly where you are, and that's okay. These are features that are explicitly banned from Alongside — not because they're absent, but because they have been actively considered and deliberately removed.

Guilt notifications"You haven't worked out in 3 days" increases avoidance, not engagementWarm welcome-back message; no commentary on absence

Single directive ("do this workout")Triggers demand avoidance in autistic users; removes autonomyThree options always presented; user chooses
Black-box recommendationsBreaks trust; prevents coaching literacy developmentEvery suggestion explained with transparent rationale

The bigger question

Alongside applies Universal Design for Learning principles to fitness — the idea that designing for the most complex needs produces something that works better for everyone. If you build for a neurodivergent adult with ADHD and variable energy, you end up with something that works better for a person in burnout, a person observing Ramadan, a person in perimenopause, a person with a chronic condition.

The question we're beginning to ask, and hope to explore in partnership with academic institutions, is whether this design philosophy — starting from the margins rather than the assumed default user — produces measurably better outcomes at scale. We believe it does. We're building the evidence to show it.

🔒

Where your personal data is stored

Alongside stores all your data — check-in history, workout logs, preferences, and settings — in your browser's localStorage on your own device. This means:

  • Your data never leaves your device. It is not sent to any server.
  • Alongside has no access to your personal information.
  • Your data persists between sessions on the same device and browser.
  • If you clear your browser's site data or switch to a different browser, your local data will be cleared. We recommend exporting your data periodically (Settings → Export) to keep a backup.
  • If you install the app on multiple devices, each device maintains its own separate data.

This is a deliberate design decision, not a limitation. Your health and wellbeing data is yours. We don't want it, and we've built the app so we can't have it.
