Our Approach

Listen first.
Prescribe second.

The mainstream coaching app has a fatal flaw: it's built for the best version of you. The one with consistent energy, clear goals, and unlimited motivation. But real life — especially for neurodivergent adults — doesn't work like that.

Build New Habits starts somewhere different. Every product begins by asking how you're actually doing today. It adapts to your reality, not the reality it wishes you had.

"Compassionate digital coaching for the people mainstream apps have always failed."

— Graeme Wright, Founder
🚫

No shame mechanics

No streaks to break. No guilt notifications. No punishment for having a hard day. A fresh start is always available — because life is genuinely hard sometimes.

💡

Radical transparency

Every recommendation comes with a clear "why." Not a black box. Users — especially neurodivergent users — deserve to understand the logic behind every suggestion.

🧠

Evidence-based design

Grounded in Self-Determination Theory, SOLO Taxonomy, Mood Meter science, and Dr. Claire Plumbly's burnout research. Not trends. Evidence.

🛡️

Privacy by default

All data stays on your device. No accounts required. No third-party tracking. No selling your behaviour to advertisers. Your data is yours.

The Evidence Base

Not trends. Science.

Every design decision in the Build New Habits platform is traceable to a specific psychological or educational framework. This isn't wellness content dressed up as coaching — it's instructional design applied to habit formation and behaviour change.

Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan)

Autonomy, competence, and relatedness underpin every feature. Users choose — they're never commanded.

SOLO Taxonomy (Biggs & Collis)

Scaffolded learning progression from surface to deep engagement, applied to habit formation.

Mood Meter Science (Yale Centre for EI)

Energy × Pleasantness check-ins map to the Mood Meter quadrant model for emotional awareness.

Burnout Research (Dr. Claire Plumbly)

"Burnout: How to Manage Your Nervous System" — detection patterns built directly from this research.

Acceptance & Commitment Therapy

Psychological flexibility and values-based action guide the coaching language and framing.
